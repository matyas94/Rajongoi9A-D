Biography

Orbán Viktor was born on May 31, 1963, in Székesfehérvár. He proved to be a talented student in his youth and participated in numerous school competitions. In 1987, he earned a law degree from Eötvös Loránd University in Budapest.

He began his political career during the regime change, when he started to engage in politics as a member of the Youth Democratic Forum (IDF) in 1988. He was a founding member of Fidesz and, after the first free elections in 1993, the party merged with the Hungarian Democratic Forum to become a prominent figure of the center-right movement.

Orbán served as Hungary's prime minister from 1998 to 2002, then went into opposition. He returned to the prime ministerial position in 2010 and was reaffirmed by voters in 2014, 2018, and 2022.
